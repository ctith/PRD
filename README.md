# Product requirement document

## ⏲️ Duration
30–45 min

## 🎯 Goal
Brainstorm with AI to generate, filter, and select one project idea you’ll turn into a real prototype over the next 2 days. Come out of this challenge with a clear project winner that you will then validate with your teacher.

## 🔧 Tools
Your preferred LLM chat assistant (ChatGPT, Gemini, Le Chat …)
Any note-taking app (Notion recommended)

## 📃 Instructions
### Step 1 - Write your builder profile (5 min)
Before prompting any AI, give it the context it needs to generate relevant ideas. Write on your note taking app:

Domains you know or work in (finance, healthcare, education, HR, etc.)
Target users you can reach or easily imagine (developers, students, small business owners, etc.)
Personal interests that could shape the product (AI, sustainability, productivity, security, etc.)
Constraints to keep in mind (data privacy, no paid APIs, no complex infra, etc.)
Keep it short - 5 to 10 bullet points in total is enough.


### Step 2 - AI-assisted idea generation (10 min)
Pick one of the prompts below, fill in your details from Step 1, and send it to your AI-assistant:

“Given my background in [domain/role], list 10 narrow, operational pains faced by [target user] solvable with a single-screen or single-workflow POC in less than 2 days.”
“Generate 5 problem statements in [industry] where a very small POC could deliver measurable value this week. Include the current workaround and why now is a good time to tackle the problem.”
“From recent forum/review chatter, what recurring frustrations do [role] have with [tool/process]? Suggest a tiny product slice and how to measure success tomorrow.”
Capture 5 to 10 raw ideas. Do not filter them yet.


### Step 3 - Shortlist to 3 candidates
Go through your list and for each idea, quickly check:
- Can you clearly identify a user and a pain?
- Is there a realistic AI use case in this product?
- Could a working demo be built in under 2 days?

Discard any idea that fails on one of these points and keep only those that pass all three.

Then score your top candidates across 3 axes: Impact x Feasibility x Personal interest. Rate each idea from 1 to 3 on each category and multiply them.
Example: Idea A scores 3×1×3 = 9 (exciting but not buildable in time). Idea B scores 2×3×2 = 12. Idea B wins: its strong feasibility makes it the safer bet.

Keep the 3 highest rated ideas and ask an AI to help you sanity-check them: existing alternatives, data availability, obvious blockers.

### Step 4 - Commit to one
Pick your favourite idea among the 3 best ones and write 2-3 sentences covering:

Who the user is
What problem they face
What the solution direction could be
You will use these to validate your idea with your teacher and it will be your starting point for writing the PRD tomorrow.

Example: “Freelance designers waste time manually chasing invoice payment statuses across emails and Notion. The product is a lightweight dashboard that aggregates payment status across clients and sends smart follow-up reminders. The AI layer auto-drafts follow-up messages based on invoice age and client history.”

## 📦 Deliverable
Make sure you end your day with 1 chosen idea validated by your teacher.


## 🥇 Key Learning Points
Structured AI-assisted brainstorming produces better, more relevant ideas faster than thinking alone.
The best project idea is not the most complex one, it is the one solving a real, specific pain for a real user.
Committing to an idea early gives you more time to build and iterate.
